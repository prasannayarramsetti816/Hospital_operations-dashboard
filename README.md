#HOSPITAL OPERATIONS DASHBOARD
#📌 Project Overview
This project presents a Hospital Operations Dashboard built using Power BI to analyze and visualize key hospital metrics.
The dashboard consolidates patient, revenue, and operational data into a single interactive view for quick decision-making.

#📚 About the Dataset
The dataset (Hospital_Data table) contains hospital operations records at the patient level.
It includes the following fields:
Patient_ID, Age, Gender, City, Department, Diagnosis, Doctor_Name
Admission_Date, Discharge_Date, Length_of_Stay
Hospital_Revenue, Treatment_Cost, Insurance_Coverage, Payment_Mode
The data spans multiple years (2022–2024), enabling year-over-year comparison of patient visits and admissions.
Diagnosis categories include Diabetes, Infection, Cancer, Fracture, and Heart Disease.
Payment modes include Card, Insurance, and UPI.
Departments include Cardiology, General Medicine, and Neurology.

#🤖 Why Power BI?
Power BI enables interactive, drag-and-drop visualization of large operational datasets without heavy coding.
Slicers and filters allow real-time exploration of data by department, city, or year.
DAX measures support quick calculation of KPIs like averages, sums, and counts.
Power BI dashboards are easily shareable and refreshable, making them ideal for ongoing hospital reporting.

#🔍 Steps Followed in This Project
Data Loading and Preparation
Imported hospital operations data into Power BI.
Cleaned and structured fields for diagnosis, payment method, city, and department.
Data Modeling
Built relationships between tables where required.
Created calculated measures using DAX (e.g., Sum of Revenue, Average Age, Average Insurance).
Dashboard Design
Designed KPI cards for high-level metrics.
Added charts for distribution and trend analysis.
Included slicers for interactive filtering.
Visualization
Used donut, bar, area, and pie charts to represent different aspects of hospital data.
Interactivity
Added a Department Slicer to filter all visuals by Cardiology, General Medicine, or Neurology.

#📊 Key Insights from Visualization
Total hospital revenue and average patient age give a quick snapshot of overall performance.
Patient distribution by diagnosis highlights the most common conditions treated.
Annual patient visits show trends in patient volume across years.
Admissions by city reveal geographic patterns in patient intake.
Patient gender distribution and payment method breakdown support operational and financial planning.
